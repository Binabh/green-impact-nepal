R-Basics Training Program

Week-long intensive training on R programming and data analysis

Completed Project August 4-9, 2024

Training Overview

Green Impact Nepal organized a week-long training program on R-Basics from 4th-9th August, 2024. The resource person for the training was Mr. Prakash Lamichhane, research officer at Climate Change Management Division.

The training was held both physically at Green Impact Nepal's office in Buddhanagar, and virtually, designed to provide basic R programming skills from installation to data analysis techniques. A total of 10 participants were present in the training.

Training Structure

The training started with the chairperson welcoming all participants and briefing about the syllabus. The program began with an introduction to R and RStudio, where participants learned to install the software and navigate its interface.

The training moved on to the first logical entry point in programming: learning about basic syntax, variables, and operators with R. In the following days, participants learned about different data types, including vectors, lists, matrices, and data frames, and practiced manipulating data sets using built-in R functions.

Curriculum Highlights

Core Topics Covered

  • ✓ R and RStudio installation and setup
  • ✓ Basic syntax, variables, and operators
  • ✓ Data types: vectors, lists, matrices, and data frames
  • ✓ Data manipulation using built-in functions

Practical Skills

  • ✓ Reading and writing files in different formats
  • ✓ Basic data analysis techniques
  • ✓ Hands-on exercises and practical applications
  • ✓ Problem-solving with real datasets

Training Impact

The training also involved reading and writing files in different formats, which is an essential ability for data analysis. Each day included hands-on exercises, ensuring that participants could apply what they learned immediately.

The training concluded with a vote of thanks from the Chairperson of Green Impact Nepal, expressing gratitude to the participants, the trainer, and everyone involved in making the program a success.
